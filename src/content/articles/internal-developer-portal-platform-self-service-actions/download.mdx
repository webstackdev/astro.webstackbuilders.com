A developer spent three days getting a PostgreSQL database. The company had a beautiful internal developer portal with service catalog and scorecards. He found the database docs in 30 seconds. Then the instructions: file a Jira ticket, wait for triage, attend a review meeting to justify the request, receive credentials via another ticket. Three to five business days. The portal worked perfectly. The developer still waited the same time. A portal indexes things: links, documentation, ownership. A platform does things: provisions infrastructure, creates repositories, grants access. Most organizations plateau at building a directory instead of a platform. Without self-service actions, you've built a prettier wiki.

This complete guide teaches you how to transform your portal into an actual platform.
